Japanese Flashcard Website

My Japanese Flashcard site is designed to help learners quickly choose pre-existing questions and test themselves. Although users can test anonymously, they can sign in through their Gmail account to keep track of which questions they get right and wrong. Currently, users can choose to test their vocabulary over different Japanese levels, or the basic Japanese alphabet.

Future Features A list of features that I plan to implement

Statistics

Currently user statistics on which questions they get correct and incorrect are saved, and associated with their Gmail usernames. I plan on implementing a page allowing users to see these stats, and options to create tests consisting of questions the user frequently gets incorrect.

Google Vision

Vocabulary sheets are commonplace when learning Japanese, and users aren't always able to find sites that offer tests for their specific sets of words. Letting users upload images of their sheets, using Google's Vision API to extract Japanese words, and matching the words that were caught with the pre-existing terms in my database lets users quiz themselves immediately.

Conjugation Tables

One of the reasons why I love Japanese from a programming perspective is that conjugations are completely predictable. Although it's predictable, a lot of beginners struggle with understanding and memorizing conjugation rules, which is why I want to make conjugation tables for a myriad of different conjugations.
