Nice to Meet You! Welcome to my personal site! This is just going to be a hub where I link some of my personal projects I've worked on over the past couple of years (source code included ツ).
I first started programming in my first year of high school, starting off with C++, and going into Java for my second and third year. Having my programs come to life felt rewarding, and building them were incredibly fun! With the additional support in tuition I earned from competitions, I decided to major in computer science in the University of Calgary.
The most influential decision I came to make in university was my choice to take Japanese language classes. I made tons of good memories taking as many Japanese language classes as I could, such as making great friends and volunteering as a Teaching Assistant for lower level classes, and conversing with others in Japanese was exciting, even though a lot of conversations were definitely lost in translation! Despite language classes taking up more time to study for than all my other classes combined, I'm proud that I stuck with it through my degree.
For my fourth year I did a 16 month internship, making medical-based applications with TypeScript and Angular. It was startling to realize how little my university classes did to prepare me for my internship, since I had no technical experience with real web development at the time. Fortunately for me, my boss was patient and understanding as I stumbled around my first month, and I was able to pick up TypeScript and Angular, learn to use git through the CLI, and a lot more. As I started getting into the groove of working, I looked for Japanese tutors and took one on one lessons Tuesdays and Thursdays, so my Japanese would not get rusty during my hiatus away from university classes. Overall, my internship experience was extremely helpful, and I wish internships were more readily available for students to prepare them for their future jobs.
